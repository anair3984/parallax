La Historia

History

Madrid, city, capital of Spain and of Madrid provincia (province). Spain’s arts and financial center, the city proper and province form a comunidad autónoma (autonomous community) in central Spain. Madrid lies almost exactly at the geographical heart of the Iberian Peninsula. It is situated on an undulating plateau of sand and clay known as the Meseta. Madrid was officially made the national capital by Philip III, an entire generation after Philip II took the court to Madrid in 1561.

La Comida

The Food

Madrid has many popular food items with many diverse ingredients. They have the Cocido Madrileno, it is a pork stew it has chickpeas, meats,vegetables and pork. The typical way to eat cocido madrileño is in two or three courses. Once the chickpeas, meats, and vegetables are finished cooking, cooks separate the broth and use it to make soup with small vermicelli noodles. This steaming soup becomes the first course.The next dish is Hueves Rotos This typical Madrid dish is a plate of potatoes which are freshly fried in Spanish olive oil and tossed with sea salt. The potatoes are topped with perfect over-easy eggs. Depending on where you go, you’ll either break the yolks with the edge of a crusty piece of bread or your server will break them, often at the table.
